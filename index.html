An independent snapshot of which local businesses are currently visible to AI-driven search and recommendation systems.

AI systems now summarise, rank, and recommend businesses automatically. This index documents which local businesses are currently interpreted as visible or invisible by those systems, based on public-facing signals only.

  • This is not a review of quality
  • This is not advertising
  • This reflects observable digital signals at the time of assessment

About This Index

The Local Business AI Visibility Index is an independent research initiative that monitors how local businesses are represented to AI-driven discovery systems. It operates without commercial relationships with any listed business.

Independence

No business can pay to be listed, removed, or reclassified. All assessments are conducted using publicly available information only.

Assessment Cycle

Businesses are reviewed on a rolling basis. Each entry reflects signals observed at the time of assessment, not real-time status.

Scope

This index currently covers local service businesses operating in the United Kingdom. Geographic expansion is under consideration.

Current Classifications

Businesses are assigned one of two classifications based on clarity, accessibility, and machine-readable signals.

Visible

  • Clear services
  • Direct booking or contact
  • Structured public signals
  • Consistent presence

Invisible

  • Unclear offerings
  • Broken or slow contact paths
  • Weak or missing signals
  • Outperformed by nearby competitors

Methodology

Classifications are determined through structured analysis of publicly observable signals. The assessment process does not involve proprietary data, paid tools, or private information.

01

Signal Collection

Public-facing digital presence is analysed across search engines, directories, and structured data sources.

02

Clarity Assessment

Service descriptions, contact pathways, and booking mechanisms are evaluated for machine interpretability.

03

Consistency Check

Information accuracy and consistency across platforms is verified against observable standards.

04

Classification

A binary classification is assigned based on whether signals meet the threshold for reliable AI interpretation.

Why This Matters

As AI assistants increasingly replace first clicks and manual search, visibility is no longer optional. Businesses that are not clearly interpretable risk being silently bypassed.

  • Lost recommendations do not generate warnings
  • Visibility compounds over time
  • Inaction rarely self-corrects
