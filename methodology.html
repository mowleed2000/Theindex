Methodology

How assessments are conducted and classifications are determined.

Assessment Overview

The Local Business AI Visibility Index assesses local businesses based on the interpretability of their publicly observable digital signals. Assessments are conducted using information that is freely available to automated systems.

This methodology documents what is assessed, what is not assessed, and how classifications are determined.

What Is Assessed

Assessments examine the following categories of publicly observable signals.

01

Business Information Consistency

Whether core business details (name, address, contact information) are consistent across public platforms and sources.

02

Service Description Clarity

Whether the nature of services offered is clearly and unambiguously described in publicly accessible formats.

03

Machine-Readable Location Signals

Whether location information is presented in formats that automated systems can reliably interpret.

04

Platform Redundancy

Whether business information is present across multiple public platforms, reducing dependency on any single source.

05

Third-Party Reference Coherence

Whether references to the business from external sources are consistent with the business's stated information.

What Is Not Assessed

The following factors are explicitly excluded from assessment.

Quality of Service

The index does not assess, rate, or rank the quality of products or services delivered by any business.

Customer Reviews

Review scores, customer feedback, and reputation data are not factors in classification decisions.

Business Performance

Financial performance, growth, or commercial success are not considered in assessments.

Proprietary Systems

The index does not access private databases, internal systems, or non-public information of any kind.

Regulatory Compliance

Licensing, certifications, and regulatory status are not assessed or verified.

Advertising Activity

Paid advertising, promotional activity, or marketing spend are not factors in any assessment.

Classification Process

Each assessed business is assigned one of three classifications based on signal analysis.

Clear

Public signals allow consistent automated interpretation. The business is reliably identifiable and categorisable by AI systems.

Ambiguous

Signals exist but introduce uncertainty or misclassification risk. The business may be inconsistently represented across platforms.

Uninterpretable

Signals are insufficient for reliable automated interpretation. The business may be overlooked or misrepresented by AI systems.

Classification reflects interpretability of public signals by automated systems. It is not a measure of quality, reputation, or compliance.

Data Sources

All assessments are conducted using publicly accessible information sources. These may include:

  • Public business websites
  • Public business directories
  • Publicly indexed web pages
  • Structured data in public formats
  • Third-party references in public documents

The index does not access private systems, paid databases, or restricted sources of any kind.

Limitations

This methodology is designed to provide a point-in-time assessment based on observable signals. The following limitations apply:

  • Temporal scope: Classifications reflect signals at the time of assessment and may not reflect current status.
  • Signal availability: Businesses with limited public presence may have fewer signals available for assessment.
  • Interpretation variance: Different AI systems may interpret the same signals differently.
  • No guarantee: Classification does not guarantee any particular outcome in AI-driven discovery systems.

This index reflects an independent assessment of publicly available information. It does not constitute endorsement, certification, or guarantee of business quality or service delivery.
